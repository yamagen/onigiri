Onigiri Quiz: Test Your Knowledge About Onigiri!

  1. Q1: What is the main ingredient in onigiri?
    • A. Bread
    • B. Rice
    • C. Noodles
    Answer B. Rice
    Onigiri is made primarily from rice, often shaped into triangles or ovals.
  2. Q2: Which of the following is a common filling for onigiri?
    • A. Umeboshi
    • B. Chocolate
    • C. Lettuce
    Answer A. Umeboshi
    Umeboshi (pickled plum) is a traditional and popular filling for onigiri.
  3. Q3: What shape is onigiri typically formed into?
    • A. Circle
    • B. Triangle
    • C. Square
    Answer B. Triangle
    Onigiri is commonly shaped into triangles, although other shapes exist.
  4. Q4: Onigiri and sushi are essentially the same food. True or False?
    Answer False
    Both use rice, but sushi rice is seasoned with vinegar, while onigiri rice is plain.
  5. Q5: It is polite to eat onigiri with chopsticks in formal situations. True or False?
    Answer False
    Onigiri is traditionally eaten with hands. Chopsticks are optional.
  6. Q6: What is the main purpose of putting fillings like umeboshi (pickled plum) inside onigiri?
    • A. Decoration
    • B. To make it sweet
    • C. For preservation and food safety
    • D. Just for taste variety
    Answer C. For preservation and food safety
    Traditionally, fillings like umeboshi or salted salmon helped prevent spoilage.
  7. Q7: Which of the following is NOT a common filling for onigiri?
    • A. Umeboshi (pickled plum)
    • B. Salmon
    • C. Chocolate
    • D. Tuna with mayonnaise
    Answer C. Chocolate
    While sweet fillings exist, chocolate is not a traditional or common filling for onigiri.
  8. Q8: Multiple Choice What shape is onigiri traditionally formed into?
    • A. Circle
    • B. Triangle
    • C. Square
    • D. Star
    Answer B. Triangle
    Onigiri is commonly shaped into triangles, although other shapes exist.
  9. Q9: In the past, rice was paid to the government as a form of tax in Japan. True or False?
    Answer True
    Before money was common, rice was used instead of money and paid as tax. This tax rice was called nengu-mai (年貢米), and the system continued until the Edo period.
  10. Q10: Multiple Choice Which prefecture usually ranks among the top rice producers in Japan?
    • A. Okinawa
    • B. Niigata
    • C. Hokkaido
    • D. Yamagata
    Answer B. Niigata
    Niigata is especially famous for its rice production, along with Hokkaido and Yamagata.
