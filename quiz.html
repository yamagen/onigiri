Onigiri Quiz: Test Your Knowledge About Onigiri!

  1. Q1: What is the main ingredient in onigiri?
    • A. Bread
    • B. Rice
    • C. Noodles
    Answer B. Rice
    Onigiri is made primarily from rice, often shaped into triangles or ovals.
  2. Q2: Which of the following is NOT a common filling for onigiri?
    • A. Umeboshi (pickled plum)
    • B. Salmon
    • C. Chocolate
    • D. Tuna with mayonnaise
    Answer C. Chocolate
    While sweet fillings exist, chocolate is not a traditional or common filling for onigiri.
  3. Q3: Multiple Choice What shape is onigiri traditionally formed into?
    • A. Circle
    • B. Triangle
    • C. Square
    • D. Star
    Answer B. Triangle
    Onigiri is commonly shaped into triangles, although other shapes exist.
  4. Q4: Onigiri and sushi are essentially the same food. True or False?
    Answer False
    Both use rice, but sushi rice is seasoned with vinegar, while onigiri rice is plain.
  5. Q5: It is polite to eat onigiri with chopsticks in formal situations. True or False?
    Answer False
    Onigiri is traditionally eaten with hands. Chopsticks are optional.
  6. Q6: What is the main purpose of putting fillings like umeboshi (pickled plum) inside onigiri?
    • A. Decoration
    • B. To make it sweet
    • C. For preservation and food safety
    • D. Just for taste variety
    Answer C. For preservation and food safety
    Traditionally, fillings like umeboshi or salted salmon helped prevent spoilage.
  7. Q7: In the past, rice was paid to the government as a form of tax in Japan. True or False?
    Answer True
    Before money was common, rice was used instead of money and paid as tax. This tax rice was called nengu-mai (年貢米), and the system continued until the Edo period.
  8. Q8: Multiple Choice Which prefecture usually ranks among the top rice producers in Japan?
    • A. Okinawa
    • B. Niigata
    • C. Hokkaido
    • D. Yamagata
    Answer B. Niigata
    Niigata is especially famous for its rice production, along with Hokkaido and Yamagata.
  9. Q9: Which of the following expressions are actually used in Japanese?
    • A. 古古米（kokomai）rice harvested two years ago
    • B. 古古古米（kokokomai）rice harvested three years ago
    • C. 超古米（chōkomai / choukomai）very, very old rice
    • D. 三年米（sannenmai）rice that is three years old
    Answer The correct answers are A, B, C, and D. All of these expressions are used in Japanese to refer to rice that is old or has been stored for a long time.m, but they have different nuances regarding the age of the rice.
  10. Q10: In which seasons are rice typically planted and harvested in Japan?
    • A. Planting rice → Spring / Harvest → Autumn
    • B. Planting rice → Summer / Harvest → Winter
    • C. Planting rice → Winter / Harvest → Summer
    • D. Planting rice → Autumn / Harvest → Spring
    Answer A. Planting rice → Spring / Harvest → Autumn
    In Japan, rice is usually planted in spring (around May) and harvested in autumn (around September to October)."Nimosaku" (二毛作) is a system where two crops are grown in a year, such as rice in summer and wheat or barley in winter.
